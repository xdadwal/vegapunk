Hey team! Here's the plan for a chill monsoon weekend in Kasauli -

THE VIBE
--------
Kasauli is a quiet British-era hill station at ~6,000 ft in the
Shivalik hills. July is off-season monsoon: green, misty, uncrowded,
and cheaper. Expect ~24-30C, occasional showers, and cool evenings.
Think slow walks, chai with a view, and no packed tourist crowds.

WHAT TO PACK
------------
- Light jacket / hoodie (evenings get cool)
- Umbrella or rain shell (monsoon showers)
- Comfortable walking shoes with grip (trails can be damp)
- A book / cards for lazy cafe afternoons


GETTING THERE (Saturday morning)
--------------------------------
- ~290 km from Delhi via NH44, about 5-5.5 hours drive.
- Leave early (~6 AM) to beat traffic and reach by lunch.
- Drive easy on the ghat sections past Parwanoo - misty bends.

  ROUTE SKETCH (Delhi -> Kasauli, ~290 km)

    [DELHI]
       |  NH44, ~250 km (~4 hrs)
       v

  Return: reverse the same route, Kasauli -> Delhi.


---------------------------------------------------------------
 THE ITINERARY
---------------------------------------------------------------

DAY 1 (Saturday) - Settle & Stroll
----------------------------------
- Reach Kasauli, check in, lazy lunch at the hotel.
- Evening walk on The Mall Road & Upper Mall - cool air, pine
  forest, no traffic.
- Sunset at Sunset Point / Lover's Lane - easy, quiet forest walk.
- Dinner + live music at the Hangout Rooftop Bar (at our hotel).

DAY 2 (Sunday) - Views & Easy Sights
------------------------------------
- Morning: Christ Church and a slow amble around the colonial town.
- Gilbert Trail - flat, scenic 1.5 km nature walk, great for birds
  and mist views.
- Optional detour: Kasauli Brewery area / Baba Balak Nath viewpoint.
- Early lunch, then start back to Delhi by ~2-3 PM to reach before
  late night.

Tip: Keep it unhurried - Kasauli's charm is in slow walks and chai
with a view.

Optional extensions (if we add a day): Sanawar, or a Chail add-on.
